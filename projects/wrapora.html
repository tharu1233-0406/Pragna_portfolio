Project 01 / Recovery wear

Wrapora

Wrapora imagines recovery wear as a protective emotional layer. The collection uses cocoon and wrap silhouettes to create garments that feel soft, breathable, and gently held rather than constrained.

Concept

This project was developed around the idea of post-workout comfort. Instead of athletic intensity, the focus shifts to the quieter state after movement: warmth, ease, recovery, and softness against the body.

Design language

Wrap silhouettes become a form of rest.

Oversized collars, shawl-like wraps, thumb-hole cuffs, high-low hems, and fluid lower volumes build a visual language that feels calm and body-aware. The final range balances performance utility with softness and a low-stress drape.

Material direction

Polyester spandex jersey and knit structures support stretch, breathability, wrinkle resistance, and easy maintenance. The material choices reflect the project's focus on medium-weight comfort with a smooth, supportive fall.

User feeling

The collection is designed for yoga practitioners, fitness lovers, and wellness-minded users who want garments that help the body transition gently from activity into recovery.

Wrapora is less about performance display and more about what clothing can do once the performance ends.
